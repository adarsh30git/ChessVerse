History of chess

Chess began in India around the 6th century, where it was called Chaturanga. It spread to Persia (as Shatranj) and then to Europe, evolving into the modern game by the 15th century. The first official tournament took place in 1851, and since then, chess has grown into a global sport played both on boards and online. It remains a symbol of strategy, intelligence, and timeless challenge.
